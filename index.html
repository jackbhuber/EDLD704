Description of the Course

The purpose of this course is to teach you about research methods and instruments for collecting data. Following the assigned readings, the course will expose you to several research methods and offer guidance for collecting both quantitative and qualitative data.

The knowledge, skill, and abilities you gain from this course will pay dividends in EDLD 707a/b: Action Plan for Problem of Practice and beyond. Think of these methods as tools for your toolbox you may use down the road whether you decide to do research, action research, or improvement science.

You should finish the course with enhanced knowledge and skill for:

  • how to select an appropriate research method for investigating a question for a study arising from a problem of practice,

  • how to conceptualize, develop, and test an instrument for collecting data,

  • how to evaluate the quality of data collected, and

  • how to express your evidence-based argument in clear, simple prose, using APA format, to a skeptical reader.
